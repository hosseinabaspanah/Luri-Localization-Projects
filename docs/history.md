# History of Luri Software Localization Activities

This repository is created to document and preserve a part of the history of the Luri language’s presence in software and digital environments. It contains translated projects, activity history, and official release records of projects in which the Luri language has been officially introduced.

---

## Overview

This collection is the result of localization and translation efforts carried out by Hossein Abaspanah in the field of software localization into the Luri language. These activities began in 2021 in a fully voluntary, independent, and individual capacity, without any institutional, organizational, corporate, or group support.

At the beginning of this path, there was no organized team or structured effort for Luri localization in software, and these activities were initiated and driven personally through individual effort.

---

## Motivation

The main motivation for starting this journey was to enable the use of software in the Luri language. At that time, the presence of Luri in well-known software and services was extremely limited, and in practice, most Luri speakers had no real access to using widely used applications in their native language.

Additionally, among part of the younger generation, a noticeable distance from the native language and a decline in its daily and digital usage could be observed.

Therefore, these activities were not merely translation tasks, but an effort to preserve, strengthen, and enable the use of the Luri language within technological environments.

---

## Early Activity

The first recorded public activity in this path dates back to August 2021, when the Luri translation for Telegram was publicly introduced through the official translation platform of the messaging service.

This experience marked the beginning of a deeper involvement in software localization. The language used in this project was primarily based on Minjayi Luri, while efforts were made to incorporate commonly used and widely understood vocabulary across different Luri varieties.

---

## Infrastructure Development

After this experience, the work expanded beyond a single translation project into exploring localization platforms and infrastructure.

One of the key milestones in this process was the effort to add Luri Bakhtiari to the Transifex platform. This process took approximately five months and was officially completed on December 24, 2022, when the addition of Luri Bakhtiari to Transifex was announced.

This event was not merely the addition of a language to a platform, but the creation of infrastructure that enabled Luri to be used in various software localization projects.

On February 22, 2023, the first translated project in Luri Bakhtiari on Transifex was introduced. This project was Lucky Patcher, marking a new stage in localization activities.

On April 12, 2023, the addition of Northern Luri and Southern Luri to the Transifex platform was also announced. During this period, a request was submitted to enable right-to-left (RTL) support for Luri languages in Weblate, in order to improve usability in localization environments.

---

## Challenges

Throughout this journey, one of the most significant challenges was not technical, but rather the prevailing perception of the Luri language.

It was often argued that due to the diversity of Luri dialects and the lack of a unified writing system, software localization into Luri was either impossible or not worthwhile.

However, experience with other languages showed that linguistic variation is not unique to Luri, and even standardized languages often use multiple equivalent terms and expressions in software localization.

This demonstrated that the absence of complete uniformity is not a barrier to a language’s presence in technology.

## Main Outcome

The most important outcome of these efforts has been the introduction of the Luri language into official and publicly usable software.

This achievement is not merely a collection of translations, but the result of an individual, voluntary effort to demonstrate that the Luri language has the capacity to exist and function within modern digital environments, alongside other global languages.

---

## Purpose of This Repository

This repository exists to preserve and document this historical process — a process that began at a time when the digital presence of the Luri language was extremely limited and gradually developed into its inclusion in several well-known software platforms.

It is hoped that these records will be useful for researchers, developers, and enthusiasts in the future, and demonstrate that individual efforts can play a meaningful role in bringing a language into digital environments.

Ultimately, the goal of this path is to encourage future generations to use their native language more actively in digital spaces and to continue the presence of the Luri language in modern technology.
